Chepo Town

Chagres National Park

Panama city, Banking zone, Via espana

Embark on an exciting guided tour of the Chagres National Park, situated close to Chepo, which is renowned for its rich biodiversity and mesmerizing natural beauty. The park is home to a wide range of species of wildlife, including monkeys, sloths, jaguars, and countless bird species.

A guided tour of the park provides a chance to explore the trails and observe the animals in their natural habitat. Enjoy birdwatching as you traverse through the park's many scenic trails, and learn about the rich history and cultural significance of the park from your knowledgeable guide.

Hiking is also a popular activity in the park, with trails leading to hidden waterfalls and stunning vistas. Don't forget to bring a camera to capture the breathtaking beauty of the park, from its lush forests to its stunning waterways

Lake & Bayano Caves

Panama city, Calle 50

When visiting Chepo, make sure to indulge in the local cuisine. You'll find many delicious dishes that will tantalize your taste buds. One dish you should try is arroz con pollo, which is a traditional dish of chicken with rice.

It is usually prepared with onions, peppers, and a variety of spices. Another popular dish is tamales, which are made with corn dough and filled with meat or vegetables. You can also try ceviche, a dish made from raw fish marinated in lime juice and mixed with onions, peppers, and other spices.

The seafood in Chepo is fresh and delicious, and you can't leave without trying it! Don't forget to try the locally grown fruits and vegetables as well, such as plantains, yucca, and papaya, which are often used in many traditional dishes.

Feast of San Cristobal

Panama city, 53rd St ave

If you're planning a visit to Chepo, don't miss out on the chance to attend some of the town's lively festivals and events. The Feast of San Cristobal is a must-see celebration that takes place every July, featuring colorful parades, live music, and traditional dances. Another event worth checking out is the Carnaval de Chepo, which takes place during the week leading up to Ash Wednesday and includes colorful costumes, street performances, and lots of dancing.

These events are a great way to experience the local culture and traditions of Chepo, and provide a unique opportunity to connect with the friendly and welcoming residents. Be sure to check the dates of these festivals and plan accordingly so you don't miss out on the fun.

Embera and Wounaan indigenous communities

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

If you're interested in learning about the indigenous communities of Panama, a visit to Chepo offers the opportunity to explore the history and culture of the Embera and Wounaan peoples. Take a guided tour to learn about their traditional way of life, including their unique relationship with the natural world and their belief systems.

You can also participate in cultural experiences such as traditional music and dance performances or try your hand at creating handicrafts using traditional techniques. Don't miss the chance to sample traditional foods and drinks as well. Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of these communities and gain a deeper appreciation for the diversity of Panama's people.
