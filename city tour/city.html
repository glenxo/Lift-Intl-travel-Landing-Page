All you need to know about
Panama City



Panama City, the capital of Panama in Central America, is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you're interested in history, nature, culture, or food, you'll find plenty to explore and enjoy in this welcoming city.

One of the most iconic landmarks in Panama City is the Panama Canal, an engineering marvel that connects the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans. Visitors can take a tour of the canal to learn about its history and see the ships passing through the locks. The canal is a testament to human ingenuity and a must-visit for anyone interested in engineering and transportation.

For a taste of Panama's rich history and culture, head to the historic district of Casco Viejo. This charming neighborhood features beautifully preserved colonial-era architecture, narrow streets, and a vibrant nightlife scene. Visitors can explore historic landmarks like the Cathedral of Santa Maria la Antigua and the Presidential Palace, or simply wander the streets and enjoy the colorful buildings, street art, and delicious local cuisine.

Nature lovers won't want to miss the opportunity to hike up Ancon Hill, which offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscape. You can also explore nearby parks and reserves like the National Park of Soberania or take a day trip to El Valle de Anton, a nearby town known for its hot springs and stunning scenery.

In addition to its many attractions, Panama City is also home to a thriving food scene that celebrates the country's unique culinary traditions. Try traditional dishes like sancocho (a hearty stew made with chicken and root vegetables), ceviche (a seafood dish marinated in citrus juices), or arroz con pollo (rice with chicken) at local restaurants and street food vendors. And be sure to sample some of Panama's delicious coffee, which is grown in the country's lush highlands.

Finally, no visit to Panama City would be complete without a visit to its beautiful beaches and natural scenery. The Causeway Islands, located at the entrance of the Panama Canal, offer stunning views of the city skyline and the ocean. And nearby Isla Taboga is a popular day trip destination, known for its tranquil beaches and clear blue waters.

With so much to see and do, Panama City is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a unique and exciting travel experience. Come explore the history, culture, nature, and cuisine of this beautiful city, and experience the warmth and hospitality of Panama's people for yourself.

While visiting Panama City

Things to consider when visiting the City

Panamas Climate

Panama City's tropical climate is one of its defining features, with warm temperatures and high humidity year-round. If you're planning a trip to the city, it's important to be prepared for the weather conditions.

During the summer months, temperatures can soar, so it's essential to pack lightweight, breathable clothing that will help you stay cool and comfortable throughout the day. Opt for cotton or other natural fibers that wick moisture away from your skin and allow air to circulate.

You may also want to bring a hat or other protective gear to shield you from the sun. And, of course, staying hydrated is crucial, so be sure to drink plenty of water and other fluids throughout the day. Whether you're exploring the city's historic landmarks, hiking up Ancon Hill, or lounging on the beach, staying cool and hydrated will help you make the most of your visit to Panama City.

Spanish Language

While many Panamanians speak English, it's important to note that Spanish is the country's official language, and outside of tourist areas, it's not always easy to find people who speak English. That's why it's a good idea to consider taking a Spanish language course before your trip, or at least bring along a Spanish phrasebook.

Not only will it help you communicate with locals and make navigating the city easier, but it can also enrich your cultural experience and help you gain a deeper appreciation for Panama's vibrant and diverse culture.

Speaking a few phrases in Spanish is also a great way to connect with the friendly and welcoming locals, who are always happy to help visitors discover the best that their country has to offer. So don't be shy about practicing your Spanish - you'll find that it can open doors and lead to unexpected adventures during your visit to Panama City.

Visit the Panama Canal

No visit to Panama City would be complete without a trip to the iconic Panama Canal, which is one of the most impressive engineering marvels in the world. Watching massive ships pass through the locks is truly a sight to behold, and you'll be awed by the incredible ingenuity and foresight that went into the canal's construction.

You can take a guided tour of the canal to learn more about its history and operation, or simply wander around the visitor center and take in the breathtaking views of the canal and surrounding rainforest. The Panama Canal is a testament to human achievement and perseverance, and it's a must-see attraction for anyone visiting the city. So be sure to add it to your itinerary and prepare to be amazed by this incredible feat of engineering!

Panamenian Coffee and Cuisine

Panama is known for producing some of the world's best coffee, and if you're a coffee lover, you won't want to miss the chance to try it during your visit to Panama City. The country's rich volcanic soil and unique microclimate make it the perfect place for growing high-quality coffee beans, which are hand-picked and carefully processed to create a smooth and flavorful brew.

Whether you prefer a bold espresso or a creamy cappuccino, you'll find plenty of options to satisfy your caffeine cravings. But Panama's culinary delights go far beyond coffee - the country's cuisine is a delicious blend of indigenous, Spanish, and Afro-Caribbean flavors that reflect its diverse cultural heritage. From fresh ceviche to hearty stews and rice dishes, there's something for everyone to enjoy. So be sure to sample some of Panama's traditional dishes, like sancocho (a hearty soup made with chicken, yucca, and plantains), arroz con pollo (chicken and rice), or empanadas (savory pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables).

And don't forget to indulge in some of the country's tropical fruits, like mango, papaya, and pineapple, which are often used to create refreshing juices and smoothies. With so many delicious options to choose from, you'll never go hungry during your visit to Panama City.

Cash & Supplies

It's always a good idea to plan ahead and make sure you have the necessary cash and upplies for your visit to Panama City. While many businesses do accept credit cards, it's always a good idea to carry some cash with you, especially for smaller purchases or when visiting local markets or street vendors.

You may also want to bring some basic supplies, such as sunscreen, insect repellent, and a reusable water bottle to stay hydrated throughout the day. And don't forget to pack comfortable walking shoes, as many of the city's attractions and neighborhoods are best explored on foot.

With a little bit of planning and preparation, you can ensure that your visit to Panama City is comfortable, enjoyable, and hassle-free. So pack your bags, grab your essentials, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in this vibrant and fascinating city!

In conclusion

Panama City has much to offer for visitors seeking adventure, culture, and relaxation. While preparing for your trip, keep in mind the hot and humid weather and take precautions against mosquito bites. Learning some Spanish phrases can enhance your cultural experience and help you connect with locals.

Don't miss out on the unique cuisine, the impressive engineering feat of the Panama Canal, and the charming historic district of Casco Viejo. When exploring the city, exercise caution when traveling alone or at night and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. Visit the top of Ancon Hill for stunning panoramic views, the colorful and modern Biomuseo to learn about Panama's unique biodiversity, and the nearby town of El Valle de Anton for its beautiful scenery and hot springs.

Finally, make sure to relax and enjoy the beautiful beaches and natural scenery that Panama City has to offer.
