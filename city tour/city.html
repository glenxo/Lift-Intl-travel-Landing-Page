All you need to know about Panama City

Panama City, the capital of Panama in Central America, is a vibrant and exciting
destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you're interested in history,
nature, culture, or food, you'll find plenty to explore and enjoy in this welcoming city.
One of the most iconic landmarks in Panama City is the Panama Canal,
an engineering marvel that connects the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans.
Visitors can take a tour of the canal to learn about its history
and see the ships passing through the locks.
The canal is a testament to human ingenuity and a must-visit for
anyone interested in engineering and transportation.
For a taste of Panama's rich history and culture, head to the historic
district of Casco Viejo. This charming neighborhood features beautifully preserved
colonial-era architecture, narrow streets, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
Visitors can explore historic landmarks like the Cathedral of Santa Maria la Antigua
and the Presidential Palace, or simply wander the streets and enjoy
the colorful buildings, street art, and delicious local cuisine.
Nature lovers won't want to miss the opportunity to hike up Ancon Hill,
which offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscape.
You can also explore nearby parks and reserves like the National
Park of Soberania or take a day trip to El Valle de Anton,
a nearby town known for its hot springs and stunning scenery.
In addition to its many attractions, Panama City is also home to a thriving
food scene that celebrates the country's unique culinary traditions.
Try traditional dishes like sancocho (a hearty stew made with chicken and root vegetables),
ceviche (a seafood dish marinated in citrus juices), or arroz con pollo (rice with chicken)
at local restaurants and street food vendors.
And be sure to sample some of Panama's delicious coffee, which is grown in the country's lush highlands.
Finally, no visit to Panama City would be complete without a visit
to its beautiful beaches and natural scenery. The Causeway Islands, located at the entrance
of the Panama Canal, offer stunning views of the city skyline and the ocean.
And nearby Isla Taboga is a popular day trip destination, known for its tranquil beaches and clear blue waters.
With so much to see and do, Panama City is a must-visit destination for anyone
looking for a unique and exciting travel experience. Come explore the history,
culture, nature, and cuisine of this beautiful city,
and experience the warmth and hospitality of Panama's people for yourself.

Respect their customs

When visiting the Embera "Parara Puru" Tribe in Panama, it's essential to respect their customs, beliefs, and ways of life. As visitors, we must remember that we are guests in their community and culture. Understanding and appreciating their customs can help create a positive relationship and an enjoyable experience for all. It's essential to learn about their dress code and social etiquette before visiting the tribe. For instance, women are expected to dress modestly in long skirts or pants, and men wear loincloths. Following their dress code and social etiquette demonstrates our respect for their culture and helps create a more comfortable and welcoming atmosphere. By respecting their customs, we also gain a better understanding of their way of life, their values, and beliefs. It's an opportunity to learn from them and appreciate the unique traditions of their culture. In summary, respecting the customs of the Embera "Parara Puru" Tribe is essential when visiting. It allows us to learn from and appreciate their unique culture while creating a positive and respectful relationship.

Weather

Isla Grande, Panama, has a tropical climate, which means high temperatures and humidity levels throughout the year. When visiting Isla Grande, it's essential to pack lightweight, breathable clothing to stay comfortable in the heat. It's also crucial to plan your activities accordingly to avoid the hottest part of the day, typically from midday until late afternoon. During these hours, it's best to relax in the shade or cool off in the water. In addition to packing the right clothing, it's important to bring other essentials to stay comfortable in the heat. Sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses can protect you from the strong tropical sun, while mosquito repellent can help keep pests at bay.
