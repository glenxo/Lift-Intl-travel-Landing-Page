All you need to know about
Isla Grande & Portobelo

Portobelo is a charming little town located on the northern coast of Panama, known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty. It is a popular tourist destination due to its well-preserved colonial architecture, world-class beaches, and a vibrant cultural scene.
The town was founded in the 16th century by Spanish conquistadors and quickly became an important port for transporting gold, silver, and other valuable commodities back to Europe. Today, visitors can explore the impressive fortifications and ruins that remain from this time, including the San Lorenzo Castle and the Customs House, both of which have been declared UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

But Portobelo is not just about history - it also boasts some of the most beautiful beaches in Panama, including Blanca and Isla Grande, which are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling. The town's vibrant culture is on full display during the annual Congo festival, a celebration of Afro-Caribbean music, dance, and art that draws visitors from all over the world.
Whether you are interested in history, culture, or just soaking up the sun on a beautiful beach, Portobelo has something to offer. So if you are planning a trip to Panama, make sure to put this gem of a town on your itinerary!

Bring of sunscreen and insect repellent

When visiting Portobelo, it's important to be prepared for the tropical climate and the insects that come with it. Bringing plenty of sunscreen and insect repellent is crucial for protecting your skin from the intense sun and keeping bugs at bay. The sun in Portobelo can be particularly strong, so it's important to reapply sunscreen regularly and wear protective clothing like hats and sunglasses.

Insect repellent can also help prevent mosquito bites, which can carry diseases like Zika, dengue fever, and chikungunya. By taking these simple precautions, you can enjoy all that Portobelo has to offer without worrying about sunburn or bug bites putting a damper on your trip.

Visit during the annual Congo festival

If you're planning a trip to Portobelo, you might want to consider visiting during the annual Congo festival, which takes place in October. This vibrant celebration is a testament to the region's rich Afro-Caribbean culture and is a feast for the senses. The festival features colorful parades, lively music and dance performances, and traditional food and drinks.

You'll have the opportunity to experience the unique traditions of the Congo culture, including the famous devil dances and the wearing of colorful masks and costumes. The festival is also a great opportunity to connect with locals and learn more about the area's history and cultural heritage. Don't miss out on this unforgettable experience!

Bring cash

When traveling to Portobelo, it's important to bring cash with you, as many places in the town do not accept credit cards. This includes small businesses like street vendors, as well as some restaurants and shops. While there are a few ATMs in the town, they can be unreliable and may not always have cash on hand. By bringing cash with you, you'll be able to make purchases and pay for services without worrying about finding an ATM or struggling to find a place that accepts credit cards.

It's a good idea to bring both small and large bills, as some places may not be able to make change for larger denominations. By being prepared with cash, you'll be able to fully enjoy all that Portobelo has to offer without any hassle or inconvenience.

Try some of the local cuisine

When visiting Portobelo, trying the local cuisine is a must-do. The town boasts a variety of delicious Afro-Caribbean dishes that are sure to delight your taste buds. Seafood lovers can indulge in fresh catches like lobster, shrimp, and fish, often served with rice and beans or plantains. Other local specialties include sancocho, a hearty stew made with chicken or beef, and carima√±olas, deep-fried cassava rolls filled with meat or cheese.

You can also try refreshing drinks like coconut water or chicha, a fermented corn drink. Many restaurants and street vendors in Portobelo offer these dishes and more, giving you a chance to sample the flavors of the area and experience the local culinary scene. Don't be afraid to ask locals for their recommendations - they'll be happy to share their favorite spots to eat!

Have an open mind and embrace the local culture

When visiting Portobelo, it's important to have an open mind and embrace the local culture to fully appreciate the beauty and richness of the town. Portobelo is home to a vibrant Afro-Caribbean community with a unique history and cultural heritage. By immersing yourself in the local culture, you'll gain a deeper understanding of the traditions, customs, and way of life in the area.

Take the time to explore the historic forts and churches, attend cultural festivals and events, and try local cuisine. Engage with locals and learn about their daily lives and experiences. By doing so, you'll not only gain a greater appreciation for Portobelo, but also forge meaningful connections and memories that will last a lifetime. So go ahead, embrace the local culture and enjoy everything that Portobelo has to offer!
