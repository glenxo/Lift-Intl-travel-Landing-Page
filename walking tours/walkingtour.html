All you need to know about
El Casco Viejo de Panama



Casco Antiguo, also known as Casco Viejo, is one of the most popular destinations in Panama.
It's the historical district of Panama City and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
Casco Antiguo is a melting pot of cultures, featuring beautiful colonial architecture,
historical landmarks, and a vibrant cultural scene.
One of the must-see attractions in Casco Antiguo is the Presidential Palace,
which is open to the public on weekends.
The palace features a beautiful colonial-style architecture, and visitors can take a guided tour of the building's interior.
Another must-see landmark is the Iglesia de San Jose, a beautiful church built in the 17th century. The church is home to the famous Golden Altar, a stunning piece of art made entirely of gold.
Casco Antiguo is also known for its vibrant culinary scene, with many restaurants offering
delicious Panamanian and international cuisine. Visitors can also enjoy the many bars and
cafes in the area, which are perfect for a relaxing break during a day of exploring.

Wear comfortable shoes as there is a lot of walking involved

When visiting Casco Antiguo, it's essential to wear comfortable shoes as there is a lot of walking involved. The neighborhood is a maze of narrow streets and alleys, filled with beautiful architecture, charming cafes, and shops.

Exploring this UNESCO World Heritage Site on foot is the best way to appreciate the rich history and culture it has to offer. The area is known for its colonial architecture, and the best way to see it up close is on foot. Comfortable shoes will make it easier to explore the area's many sights and landmarks, such as the beautiful Plaza de la Independencia or the Panama Canal Museum.

Wearing comfortable shoes will also allow you to enjoy the many cafes and restaurants that Casco Antiguo has to offer without feeling uncomfortable. By wearing comfortable shoes, you can fully immerse yourself in the beauty and charm of this historic neighborhood.

Take a stroll along the waterfront promenade.

The waterfront promenade in Casco Antiguo is a must-visit attraction for anyone exploring the area. It offers stunning views of the ocean and Panama City skyline, as well as a chance to experience the vibrant local culture.

Taking a leisurely stroll along the promenade is a perfect way to soak up the sights and sounds of the area, and it's a great activity for people of all ages. Whether you're a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, a walk along the waterfront promenade is an ideal way to spend an afternoon or evening. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture the breathtaking sunsets that can be seen from the promenade.

Explore the Plaza de la Independencia and take in the beautiful architecture.

The Plaza de la Independencia is one of the most iconic landmarks in Casco Antiguo, Panama. This charming square boasts beautiful colonial-style architecture and is steeped in history. It is surrounded by historic buildings, including the Presidential Palace, the National Theater, and the Cathedral of Panama.

Visitors can explore the plaza and marvel at the impressive architecture while taking in the vibrant energy of the surrounding area. It's a great place to relax and people-watch, or take some stunning photos of the surrounding buildings. A visit to the Plaza de la Independencia is a must-do when exploring Casco Antiguo, and visitors will not be disappointed by the charming atmosphere and stunning architecture of this historic square.

The Presidential Palace

The Presidential Palace is a must-visit attraction for those interested in learning about Panama's political history. Located in Casco Antiguo, the palace was constructed in the early 20th century and has been home to several Panamanian presidents. Visitors can take a guided tour of the palace to explore the beautiful architecture and learn about the country's political system.

Inside, visitors can see stunning paintings and decorations that reflect the country's history and culture. The palace also houses a museum with exhibitions on Panama's political history, including its independence from Spain and the construction of the Panama Canal. Visiting the Presidential Palace is an excellent way to learn more about Panama's past and gain a deeper understanding of its political system.

The Church of San Jose

The Church of San Jose in Casco Antiguo is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in religious art and architecture. The church is home to the famous golden altar, a stunning example of colonial-era craftsmanship. Visitors can marvel at the intricate details and admire the radiant golden hue of the altar, which has been carefully preserved over the centuries.

The church itself is also an impressive example of colonial architecture, with beautiful arches, columns, and ceilings. Whether you're a history buff, an art lover, or simply looking for a unique cultural experience, a visit to the Church of San Jose is an excellent way to spend an afternoon in Casco Antiguo. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture the beauty of this iconic church!

Shop for souvenirs at the local markets.

When in Casco Antiguo, make sure to visit the local markets to get your hands on some unique souvenirs to take back home. You'll find a variety of handicrafts, clothing, and accessories made by local artisans. Another popular market is the Mercado Nacional de Artesanias, which has a more extensive selection of souvenirs and is located in the heart of the city. Shopping at the local markets is not only a great way to support the local economy but also an opportunity to take a piece of Panama's vibrant culture back home with you.
