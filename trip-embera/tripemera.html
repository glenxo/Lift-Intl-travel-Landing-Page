All you need to know about The Panama Canal

The Panama Canal is a man-made waterway in Panama that connects the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. Completed in 1914, the canal is an engineering marvel that has since become a crucial part of international trade and commerce. Visitors to Panama can take a tour of the canal, learning about its history and observing the process of ships passing through its locks. Along the way, they can also see the lush tropical rainforests and diverse wildlife that surround the canal. Whether you're a history buff, nature lover, or just interested in experiencing something unique, a visit to the Panama Canal is a must-see for anyone traveling to Panama.
