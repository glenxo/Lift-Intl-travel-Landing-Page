All you need to know about
Panama Viejo

Panama Viejo is an important historical site located in Panama City, Panama.
It was founded in 1519 by Spanish explorers, making it the oldest European settlement
on the Pacific coast of the Americas. The city was destroyed in 1671 by the infamous
pirate Henry Morgan, and the Spanish moved their capital to a new location,
which is now known as Casco Viejo.
Today, Panama Viejo is a fascinating archaeological site that showcases the
remains of the old city. Visitors can explore the ruins of the cathedral, the convent,
and the governor's palace. There are also several museums on site that provide insight
into the history of the city, including the Museum of Panama Viejo and the Museum of Religious Art.
One of the most striking features of Panama Viejo is the Torre de la Catedral,
a tower that was once part of the old cathedral. It stands as a lone reminder of
the grandeur of the city before its destruction.
Panama Viejo is an important part of Panama's cultural heritage, and a must-see
destination for anyone interested in history and archaeology. The site is easily
accessible by public transportation, and guided tours are available for those who
want a more in-depth experience. Whether you're a history buff or just curious about
the past, a visit to Panama Viejo is sure to be a memorable experience..

Climbing the Torre de la Catedral

Climbing the Torre de la Catedral is a must-do when visiting Panama Viejo. The tower is the only remaining part of the old cathedral, and it offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding area. From the top of the tower, you can see the ruins of the old city, the Pacific Ocean, and the modern skyline of Panama City. It's a great opportunity to appreciate the history and beauty of the site from a unique vantage point. The climb up the tower can be challenging, but the view is well worth it. Don't forget to bring a camera to capture the stunning views from the top.!
