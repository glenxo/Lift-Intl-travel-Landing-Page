All you need to know about
Panama Viejo

Panama Viejo is an important historical site located in Panama City, Panama.
It was founded in 1519 by Spanish explorers, making it the oldest European settlement
on the Pacific coast of the Americas. The city was destroyed in 1671 by the infamous
pirate Henry Morgan, and the Spanish moved their capital to a new location,
which is now known as Casco Viejo.
Today, Panama Viejo is a fascinating archaeological site that showcases the
remains of the old city. Visitors can explore the ruins of the cathedral, the convent,
and the governor's palace. There are also several museums on site that provide insight
into the history of the city, including the Museum of Panama Viejo and the Museum of Religious Art.
One of the most striking features of Panama Viejo is the Torre de la Catedral,
a tower that was once part of the old cathedral. It stands as a lone reminder of
the grandeur of the city before its destruction.
Panama Viejo is an important part of Panama's cultural heritage, and a must-see
destination for anyone interested in history and archaeology. The site is easily
accessible by public transportation, and guided tours are available for those who
want a more in-depth experience. Whether you're a history buff or just curious about
the past, a visit to Panama Viejo is sure to be a memorable experience..

Climbing the Torre de la Catedral

Climbing the Torre de la Catedral is a must-do when visiting Panama Viejo. The tower is the only remaining part of the old cathedral, and it offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding area. From the top of the tower, you can see the ruins of the old city, the Pacific Ocean, and the modern skyline of Panama City. It's a great opportunity to appreciate the history and beauty of the site from a unique vantage point. The climb up the tower can be challenging, but the view is well worth it. Don't forget to bring a camera to capture the stunning views from the top.!

Museum of Panama Viejo

Visiting the Museum of Panama Viejo is an essential part of any trip to this historical site. The museum provides a fascinating insight into the history and culture of Panama City, from its founding to the present day. Exhibits include artifacts from the ancient city, such as pottery and tools, as well as displays on the Spanish conquest and the city's subsequent development.
There are also interactive exhibits and multimedia displays that bring the history of the site to life. Visiting the museum is an excellent way to deepen your understanding of Panama's rich cultural heritage and appreciate the significance of Panama Viejo. Make sure to allocate enough time to explore the museum fully and take in all the fascinating exhibits.

Tropical climate

When visiting Panama Viejo, it's essential to come prepared for the tropical climate. The site is located in a sunny and humid area, so it's crucial to bring a hat, sunscreen, and plenty of water to stay hydrated and protected from the sun. The sun can be intense,
especially during the middle of the day, so it's essential to take necessary precautions to avoid sunburn and heat exhaustion. Wearing comfortable and breathable clothing is also advisable to keep cool in the hot and humid conditions. By coming prepared for the weather, you can enjoy your visit to Panama Viejo comfortably and safely.

Panama Viejo Cultural Festival

Visiting Panama Viejo during a festival or cultural event is an excellent way to experience the vibrant local culture. There are several festivals held throughout the year that celebrate Panama's cultural heritage, such as the Carnival of Panama and the International Jazz Festival. These events offer a chance to see traditional music and dance performances, sample local cuisine, and engage with the local community.
Attending a festival or cultural event is a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in Panama's culture and make lasting memories. Be sure to check the local events calendar and plan your visit accordingly to ensure you don't miss out on any exciting cultural experiences.

Walking and Exploring

Visiting Panama Viejo is an experience that involves a lot of walking and exploring, and comfortable footwear is essential to make the most of it. The ruins of Panama Viejo cover a vast area, and visitors can spend hours exploring the paths and discovering the ancient ruins. Therefore, it's crucial to wear flat and sturdy shoes that provide

adequate support and prevent any foot injuries while navigating the terrain. Moreover, comfortable shoes will ensure that visitors can explore the site for extended periods without feeling uncomfortable or experiencing any discomfort. By wearing appropriate footwear, visitors can fully appreciate the rich history and culture of the site, without having to worry about foot pain or blisters.
In summary, wearing comfortable shoes while visiting Panama Viejo is an essential aspect that visitors should consider to have an enjoyable and memorable experience.
