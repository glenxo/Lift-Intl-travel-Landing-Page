When you visit Isla Grande

Avoid touching or stepping on coral reefs or other marine life to protect their habitats

Panama city, Banking zone, Via espana

One of the most important things to keep in mind when visiting Isla Grande is to avoid touching or stepping on coral reefs or other marine life. Coral reefs are an essential part of the ocean ecosystem, and they provide a habitat for numerous species of fish and other marine life. These delicate ecosystems can easily be damaged by human contact, and it can take years or even decades for them to recover.

Stepping on coral reefs or touching marine life can not only cause physical damage but also introduce harmful bacteria and chemicals that can harm the reef and its inhabitants. Therefore, it's important to stay alert and be mindful of your surroundings when swimming, snorkeling, or diving.
By respecting the marine life and their habitats, we can help to preserve the beauty and diversity of Isla Grande's underwater world for future generations to enjoy.

Bring cash as there are limited ATMs on the island

Panama city, Calle 50

When planning a trip to Isla Grande, it's important to remember to bring cash. The island is relatively small and has limited ATMs, so it's best to be prepared to avoid any potential inconveniences during your stay.
Cash is necessary for a variety of expenses, such as purchasing meals, souvenirs, and paying for water-based activities. Some establishments may accept credit cards, but it's always better to have cash on hand as a backup. It's also a good idea to bring smaller denominations, as larger bills may be harder to change.

Having cash readily available will ensure that you don't have to waste time searching for ATMs or worry about running out of money during your stay. So, make sure to plan accordingly and bring enough cash to cover your expenses while on the beautiful island of Isla Grande.

Apply sunscreen and insect repellent to protect your skin from the sun and bugs

Panama city, 53rd St ave

When visiting Isla Grande, it's important to protect your skin from the sun and bugs by applying sunscreen and insect repellent. The island's tropical climate and beautiful beaches make it an ideal location for outdoor activities, but it's essential to take proper precautions to avoid skin damage and bug bites.
Sunscreen with a high SPF rating should be applied regularly, especially during peak sun hours, to protect against harmful UV rays. Insect repellent will help to keep bugs at bay and prevent bites that can cause itching, irritation, or even disease.

Choosing products that are environmentally friendly and biodegradable can also help to preserve the island's natural beauty and protect marine life. By being mindful of your personal care products, you can enjoy your time on Isla Grande while also being respectful of the environment.

Enjoy the local culture and customs of the island's Afro-Panamanian community

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

One of the highlights of visiting Isla Grande is the opportunity to experience the unique culture and customs of its Afro-Panamanian community. From the vibrant music and dance to the delicious local cuisine, there's so much to explore and appreciate.
It's important to approach the local culture with an open mind and respectful attitude. Engaging with the community in a positive and curious way can lead to meaningful experiences and connections.
Consider trying local dishes, attending traditional celebrations, or purchasing handicrafts made by local artisans. By supporting the local economy and celebrating the community's culture, you can help to promote sustainable tourism and preserve the island's traditions for future generations.

Remember to always ask permission before taking photos or entering private property, and to be respectful of any customs or beliefs that may differ from your own. With an open heart and a willingness to learn, you can enjoy a truly authentic experience on Isla Grande.
