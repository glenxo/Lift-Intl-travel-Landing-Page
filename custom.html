Panama Canal

Panama city, Banking zone, Via espana

The Panama Canal is a world-renowned engineering marvel that connects the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, making it one of the most important waterways for international trade. The canal was completed in 1914 after a decade of construction, and it continues to be a vital route for global shipping today.

Visitors to the canal can witness massive ships passing through the locks, a fascinating feat of human engineering. The canal's visitor center offers interactive exhibits and a film about the history and impact of the canal. There are also guided tours available, including a partial transit tour where visitors can experience the canal firsthand.

The Panama Canal is a must-see destination for anyone interested in history, engineering, or global trade. The canal has played a significant role in shaping world history and is a testament to human ingenuity and perseverance.

Aside from its historical and engineering significance, the Panama Canal is also a beautiful natural attraction. The surrounding rainforest is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including toucans, monkeys, and crocodiles. Visitors can take a boat tour of the canal's tributaries and lakes to get up close and personal with the region's natural beauty.

Overall, the Panama Canal is a fascinating and unforgettable destination for travelers from around the world. Whether you're interested in history, engineering, or nature, the canal has something to offer everyone.

Casco Antiguo

also known as Casco Viejo, is one of the most popular destinations in Panama.
It's the historical district of Panama City and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
Casco Antiguo is a melting pot of cultures, featuring beautiful colonial architecture,
historical landmarks, and a vibrant cultural scene.
One of the must-see attractions in Casco Antiguo is the Presidential Palace,
which is open to the public on weekends.
The palace features a beautiful colonial-style architecture, and visitors can take a guided tour of the building's interior.
Another must-see landmark is the Iglesia de San Jose, a beautiful church built in the 17th century. The church is home to the famous Golden Altar, a stunning piece of art made entirely of gold.
Casco Antiguo is also known for its vibrant culinary scene, with many restaurants offering
delicious Panamanian and international cuisine. Visitors can also enjoy the many bars and
cafes in the area, which are perfect for a relaxing break during a day of exploring.

Ancon Hill

Panama city, 53rd St ave

Ancon Hill is a captivating natural landmark nestled within the bustling cityscape of Panama. Rising majestically above Panama City, this verdant hill offers a serene escape from the urban chaos. Covered in lush tropical foliage, Ancon Hill showcases the rich biodiversity of the region, with vibrant flora and fauna creating a harmonious ecosystem.
As one ascends the well-maintained trails, breathtaking panoramic views unfold, revealing the shimmering waters of the Pacific Ocean on one side and the iconic Panama Canal on the other.
The tranquil ambiance, coupled with the refreshing breeze that caresses visitors, makes Ancon Hill a cherished spot for nature lovers and avid hikers alike.
Its historical significance as the former location of a U.S. military base adds an intriguing layer to its allure, as remnants of this era can still be observed. Whether it's for a peaceful nature walk, birdwatching, or capturing panoramic photographs, Ancon Hill beckons visitors to immerse themselves in its enchanting beauty and embrace a moment of tranquility amidst the vibrant energy of Panama City.

Biomuseo

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Biomuseo, located in Panama City, is a unique and visually stunning museum dedicated to celebrating the biodiversity and natural history of Panama. Designed by the renowned architect Frank Gehry, the museum's iconic structure itself is a work of art, with its vibrant and colorful panels resembling giant sails or a cascade of leaves.
Inside the Biomuseo, visitors embark on an immersive journey through Panama's rich natural heritage. The museum's exhibits creatively blend interactive displays, audiovisual presentations, and scientific information to educate and inspire visitors of all ages. Each gallery highlights a different aspect of Panama's biodiversity, including its geological formation, diverse ecosystems, and the extraordinary array of plant and animal species found within its borders.
Biomuseo takes pride in showcasing Panama's crucial role as a bridge between the Americas, connecting and influencing the evolution of species from North and South America. Through engaging displays, visitors gain a deeper understanding of the country's unique position as a biodiversity hotspot and its significant impact on the planet's ecological balance.

Beyond its educational value, Biomuseo is an architectural masterpiece that seamlessly integrates with the surrounding natural environment. The museum is set against the backdrop of Panama Bay and offers breathtaking views of the skyline, ocean, and nearby Amador Causeway.
With its captivating architecture, immersive exhibits, and dedication to promoting environmental awareness, Biomuseo stands as a testament to Panama's natural wonders and its commitment to preserving the country's rich biodiversity for future generations.

Amador Causeway

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Amador Causeway, a picturesque stretch in Panama City, is a captivating waterfront promenade that offers a delightful escape from the urban bustle. This scenic causeway connects three small islands in the Pacific Ocean, providing visitors with breathtaking views of the city skyline and the iconic Panama Canal. Lined with palm trees, vibrant gardens, and an array of dining and entertainment options, Amador Causeway is a popular destination for leisurely strolls, cycling, and enjoying stunning sunsets over the shimmering waters. Its charm, natural beauty, and tranquil atmosphere make it a must-visit spot for both locals and tourists alike.

Metropolitan Natural Park

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

The Metropolitan Natural Park, located just outside Panama City, is a verdant oasis that offers a refreshing escape into nature. Spanning over 230 hectares, this urban park is a haven for wildlife and outdoor enthusiasts. Lush tropical forests, winding trails, and diverse ecosystems await visitors, providing opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting. The park's elevated pathways offer panoramic views of the city skyline, the Panama Canal, and the surrounding natural beauty. With its rich biodiversity, peaceful ambiance, and proximity to the city, the Metropolitan Natural Park serves as a cherished retreat for those seeking tranquility and a deeper connection with nature.

Panama Viejo Archaeological Site

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Panama Viejo Archaeological Site holds a significant place in Panama's history as the remains of the original Panama City, founded in 1519. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers a captivating glimpse into the past, with its ancient ruins and archaeological excavations. Walking through the site, visitors can explore the remnants of colonial buildings, churches, and the iconic Cathedral Tower. The site's museum showcases artifacts and provides insights into the cultural and historical importance of Panama Viejo. As one navigates the well-preserved paths, they are transported back in time, imagining the bustling trade and vibrant life that once thrived within these walls. Panama Viejo Archaeological Site stands as a testament to the country's rich heritage and serves as a captivating destination for history enthusiasts and cultural explorers alike.

Miraflores Locks

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Miraflores Locks, located on the Pacific side of the Panama Canal, is a remarkable engineering marvel and a must-visit attraction. These locks serve as a gateway for ships transiting through the Panama Canal, connecting the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans. Visitors to Miraflores Locks can witness the impressive spectacle of massive vessels being raised or lowered through the lock chambers, guided by a complex system of gates and water flow control. The on-site Visitor Center provides a wealth of information about the history, construction, and operation of the canal, allowing guests to gain a deeper understanding of this incredible feat of human ingenuity. The observation decks offer breathtaking panoramic views of the lock chambers and the surrounding canal landscape, allowing visitors to witness firsthand the intricate process of maritime navigation. Miraflores Locks is not only an educational and informative experience but also a testament to the global significance and impact of the Panama Canal.

Mercado de Mariscos

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Mercado de Mariscos, situated in Panama City, is a vibrant seafood market that immerses visitors in the flavors and lively atmosphere of Panama's maritime culture. This bustling market is renowned for its fresh catches of the day, offering an abundance of seafood delicacies from the surrounding Pacific Ocean. From succulent shrimp and delectable ceviche to mouthwatering lobster and a variety of fish, the market showcases a diverse array of culinary treasures. Visitors can browse the stalls, interact with local fishermen, and even enjoy a delicious seafood meal at the on-site restaurants. With its vibrant colors, enticing aromas, and lively ambiance, Mercado de Mariscos is a culinary haven where seafood enthusiasts can indulge in the best of Panama's maritime bounty.

Multiplaza Pacific Mall

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Multiplaza Pacific Mall, located in Panama City, is a premier shopping destination that offers a luxurious and comprehensive retail experience. Spanning multiple levels, this expansive mall is home to a wide array of local and international brands, ranging from high-end fashion boutiques to electronics stores and specialty shops. Shoppers can explore a diverse selection of designer labels, discover the latest trends, and indulge in upscale dining options. The mall also features entertainment facilities, including a cinema complex and a variety of family-friendly activities. With its modern architecture, elegant ambiance, and a vast range of shopping and entertainment options, Multiplaza Pacific Mall caters to the discerning tastes of visitors and serves as a prominent retail hub in Panama City.

Panama City Beaches

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

Panama City boasts stunning beaches that offer visitors a delightful coastal getaway. The city's beaches, lined with soft white sands and kissed by the turquoise waters of the Pacific Ocean, provide the perfect setting for relaxation and outdoor activities. Playa Santa Clara, known for its calm waters, is ideal for swimming and sunbathing. Playa Venao, located a bit further from the city, is a popular spot for surfing enthusiasts, with its consistent waves and vibrant surf culture. For a more secluded experience, Isla Taboga offers pristine beaches and a tranquil atmosphere just a short boat ride away. Whether you seek sunbathing, water sports, or simply enjoying the natural beauty of the coastline, Panama City's beaches provide a delightful retreat for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts alike.

The National Theater of Panama

Panama city, Calle 50 - Casa La Guama

The National Theater of Panama, located in the heart of Panama City, stands as a magnificent cultural icon and a testament to the country's rich artistic heritage. This historic theater, inaugurated in 1908, showcases stunning neo-classical architecture adorned with intricate details and grandeur. The theater's interior is equally impressive, featuring a lavish auditorium with ornate décor, luxurious seating, and excellent acoustics that create an immersive experience for performances. The National Theater hosts a diverse range of artistic events, including ballet, opera, theater productions, concerts, and more. It serves as a platform for local and international talents, attracting art enthusiasts and culture seekers from around the world. Stepping into the National Theater of Panama is not only a visual feast but also an opportunity to immerse oneself in the vibrant arts scene and appreciate the country's rich cultural heritage.
