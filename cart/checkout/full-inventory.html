Chame is a small town located on the Pacific coast of Panama, in Central America.
Nestled between the Pacific Ocean and the majestic mountains of the Cordillera Central, this town is known
for its stunning natural beauty, abundant wildlife, and rich history. Located just a short drive from Panama City, Los Cajones de Chame is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.
Visitors to this charming town can enjoy a wide range of activities, including hiking and birdwatching in the surrounding mountains,
swimming and sunbathing on the sandy beaches, and fishing and boating in the clear waters of the Pacific. One of the most popular attractions in Los Cajones de Chame is the Cajones de Chame National Park.
This protected area is home to a diverse array of plant and animal species, including monkeys, sloths, iguanas,
and over 300 species of birds. Visitors can explore the park on foot or by bike, taking in the breathtaking views of
the mountains and the ocean as they go. In addition to its natural beauty, Los Cajones de Chame is also home to a rich cultural heritage. The town has a long history
dating back to the pre-Columbian era, and its residents are proud of their indigenous roots. Visitors can learn about the
town's history and culture by visiting the local museums and cultural centers, or by chatting with the friendly locals. Despite its small size, Los Cajones de Chame has a thriving tourism industry, with a range of accommodation options
available to suit all budgets. From luxury hotels and resorts to cozy bed and breakfasts, there is something
for everyone in this charming town. Overall, Los Cajones de Chame is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Panama. Whether you're looking for adventure,
relaxation, or a chance to immerse yourself in local culture, this charming town has something to offer. So why wait?
